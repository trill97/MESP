Powerhead

The powerhead includes the engine which is usually not put in the water. To start an outboard motor, a person must use a pull cord which provides the spark that gets the cylinders firing on the downward stroke. Most engines are cooled by water. Parts included in the powerhead include the fuel tank, a speed control lever, the carburetor, and the reed valves.
